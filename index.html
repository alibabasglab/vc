Audio samples from "Towards Natural Cross-Lingual Voice Conversion Based on Neural TTS Models and Phonetic Posteriorgrams"

 Authors: Shengkui Zhao, Hao Wang, Trung Hieu Nguyen, Bin Ma

Abstract: This paper presents a versatile neural network-based framework for voice conversion (VC), with an emphasis on non-parallel, cross-lingual task. Motivated by the success of neural text-to-speech (TTS) models, which typically use a synthesizer network to generate vocoder features from input text, we adapt the network to generate vocoder features from a sequence of Phonetic PosteriorGrams (PPGs) and fundamental frequencies. Specifically, our framework employs LPCNet vocoder, and hence its features, to synthesize speech samples. We then demonstrate the flexibility of this framework with three different synthesizer networks, based on the architectures of Tacotron2, Transformer and FastSpeech respectively. Experiments show that our VC systems, which are separately trained on a Mandarin and an English mono-lingual speaker's corpus, achieve comparable Mean Opinion Score (MOS) to professionally recorded speech, in terms of both naturalness and speaker similarity.
